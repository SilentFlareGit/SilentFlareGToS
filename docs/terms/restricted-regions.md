## 1. Purpose and scope

1.1 This Controlled Access Policy explains when SilentFlare may limit, challenge, review, or block access to the personal blog, comment features, feeds, archives, static files, API-like endpoints, crawler access, or other blog-related resources.

1.2 Controlled access is used for legal compliance, cybersecurity, anti-abuse, anti-scraping, anti-spam, anti-crawler-abuse, network protection, and operational-risk management.

1.3 This policy applies to visitors, commenters, feed readers, archive tools, crawler operators, mirror operators, automated clients, security researchers, and any other party accessing the blog or its related resources.

## 2. Non-discrimination statement

2.1 Controlled Access Regions and controlled network sources are designated for objective legal, security, anti-abuse, crawler-control, and operational-risk reasons only.

2.2 SilentFlare does not restrict access on the basis of race, ethnicity, colour, nationality, national origin, language, religion, political opinion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, social origin, or any other protected personal characteristic.

2.3 Any access limitation applies to network origin, jurisdictional compliance risk, traffic behaviour, abuse history, automated access patterns, crawler behaviour, sanctions or provider requirements, or technical risk indicators. It is not a judgment about any individual person, protected group, culture, language, or community.

2.4 A visitor's passport, nationality, language, ethnicity, religion, or personal background must not be used as the sole reason to deny ordinary personal blog access. Where KYC1 is requested, it is used only to reduce legal, security, crawler, spam, or network-abuse risk under this policy.

## 3. Objective criteria for controlled access

3.1 A country, territory, jurisdiction, network region, IP range, ASN, hosting provider, proxy provider, VPN exit node, Tor exit node, crawler source, automated client, referrer source, or traffic source may be placed under controlled access when one or more objective factors apply.

3.2 Objective factors may include:

1. applicable local internet, content, data, platform, cybersecurity, telecommunications, censorship, registration, publishing, or user-identification rules are unclear, incompatible, burdensome, or difficult for a personal blog operator to comply with;
2. the region, network, ASN, provider, or traffic source has generated repeated scraping, spam, credential abuse, denial-of-service traffic, vulnerability scanning, mirror abuse, fake search-engine crawling, comment abuse, feed abuse, or other abnormal requests;
3. the access source is associated with anonymisation, proxying, data-centre crawling, automated scraping, bot traffic, high-volume requests, evasive user agents, or rotating IP behaviour;
4. sanctions, export-control rules, hosting-provider rules, CDN-provider rules, registrar rules, upstream network policies, abuse-desk requirements, or other infrastructure obligations require or recommend limitation;
5. SilentFlare cannot reasonably provide legal notices, user-rights handling, takedown processing, complaint handling, abuse response, or compliance responses for the relevant jurisdiction;
6. temporary blocking, rate-limiting, challenge pages, feed restrictions, robots restrictions, or manual review are necessary to protect the availability, security, integrity, or personal operation of the blog;
7. access would create disproportionate legal, administrative, security, moderation, data-protection, or operational burden for a personal, non-commercial blog operator.

3.3 Controlled access should be proportionate where reasonably possible. SilentFlare may use lighter measures, such as rate limits, CAPTCHA, proof-of-work challenge, robots restrictions, feed restrictions, temporary blocking, comment moderation, or crawler identification requirements, before using stronger measures such as long-term blocking or KYC1 review.

4.5 SilentFlare should periodically review controlled-access designations where reasonably possible and may remove or reduce restrictions when the objective risk no longer applies.

## 5. Access controls

5.1 SilentFlare may block, challenge, rate-limit, redirect, cache-limit, robots-limit, feed-limit, comment-limit, API-limit, archive-limit, or otherwise restrict controlled traffic using IP geolocation, ASN, DNS signals, hosting-provider indicators, proxy/VPN indicators, user agent, crawler behaviour, request rate, device signals, referrer signals, prior abuse records, security logs, or other objective risk indicators.

5.2 Language preference, browser locale, self-declared location, or similar signals must not be used as the sole basis for controlled access. They may only be considered together with other objective risk indicators where technically or legally relevant.

5.3 Geolocation and risk data may be imperfect. A visitor, crawler operator, researcher, or other affected party who is incorrectly blocked may contact SilentFlare and request manual review.

5.4 Access from a controlled region or controlled network source without required review may be restricted even if technical blocking temporarily fails.

## 6. KYC1 as an exception mechanism

6.1 KYC1 may be requested only as an exceptional access-control measure, including when a visitor, commenter, crawler operator, researcher, mirror operator, archive operator, or automated client:

1. accesses the blog from a Controlled Access Region;
2. triggers anti-abuse, anti-scraping, anti-spam, anti-bot, or security controls;
3. requests manual whitelisting after an IP, ASN, VPN, proxy, Tor, data-centre, crawler, or region-based limitation;
4. needs to show that access is personal, lawful, non-automated, non-abusive, or otherwise compatible with this policy;
5. seeks access to comments, feed endpoints, archives, high-rate pages, API-like endpoints, crawler allowlisting, or other restricted blog functions after being limited.

6.2 KYC1 may require one valid government-issued identity document, such as a passport, national identity card, driving licence, residence permit, or comparable document. Face or liveness verification may be requested only where reasonably necessary to prevent forged documents, impersonation, automated abuse, or repeated circumvention.

6.3 SilentFlare does not use KYC2, KYC3, proof of address, source-of-funds review, source-of-wealth review, company ownership review, or enhanced commercial due diligence for ordinary personal blog access.

6.4 KYC1 is not used to classify visitors by race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, political opinion, social origin, language, or other protected personal characteristics. It is used only to reduce legal, security, crawler, spam, impersonation, fraud, and network-abuse risk.

6.5 Completion of KYC1 does not guarantee access. SilentFlare may deny, block, rate-limit, restrict, or terminate access if the objective risk remains unacceptable or if the visitor provides false, misleading, incomplete, or unverifiable information.

6.6 KYC1 data should be handled under the blog's Data and Privacy Policy and should be limited to what is reasonably necessary for the stated anti-abuse, security, legal-compliance, or manual-review purpose.

## 7. Prohibited circumvention

7.1 A visitor, crawler, operator, or automated client must not hide or misrepresent controlled-access status using VPNs, proxies, residential proxies, data-centre proxies, Tor exit nodes, remote desktops, borrowed accounts, false documents, nominee users, false crawler identities, misleading user agents, false referrers, forged headers, or traffic relays.

7.2 A visitor, crawler, operator, or automated client must not resell, share, tunnel, proxy, relay, mirror, scrape for redistribution, bypass robots rules, bypass rate limits, or provide indirect access to the blog for a controlled region or controlled network source unless SilentFlare gives prior written permission and any required KYC1 or manual review has been completed.

7.3 Circumvention may result in blocking, rate limiting, comment removal, feed blocking, crawler blocking, allowlist removal, evidence preservation, provider reporting, authority reporting where required, and refusal of future access.

## 8. Review, correction, and exceptions

8.1 A visitor affected by controlled access may contact SilentFlare and request manual review.

8.2 SilentFlare may remove, reduce, or replace a restriction when the visitor provides sufficient information to show that the access is lawful, non-abusive, non-automated where required, and compatible with the blog's technical and legal constraints.

8.3 Exceptions are discretionary, limited, revocable, non-transferable, and may include rate limits, page limits, feed limits, crawler identification requirements, user-agent requirements, cache requirements, comment limits, additional monitoring, or periodic re-verification.

8.4 An exception granted to one visitor, IP address, crawler, ASN, organization, provider, jurisdiction, or network source does not create a right for any other visitor.

8.5 Where lighter measures are sufficient, SilentFlare may prefer rate-limiting, CAPTCHA, proof-of-work challenge, manual review, crawler restrictions, comment restrictions, feed limits, or temporary blocking over permanent blocking.

## 9. No obligation to provide access

9.1 SilentFlare is a personal blog and is not obligated to provide blog access, comment access, feed access, crawler access, archive access, API-like access, or allowlisting to any visitor, crawler, operator, jurisdiction, or network source.

9.2 SilentFlare may refuse, block, rate-limit, restrict, remove, or terminate access where legal uncertainty, provider risk, sanctions risk, crawler-abuse risk, spam risk, data-protection risk, security risk, moderation burden, or operational burden is unacceptable.
