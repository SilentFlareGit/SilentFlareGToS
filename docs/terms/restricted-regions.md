# Restricted Countries and Regions

## 1. Purpose

1.1 SilentFlare may restrict access from certain countries, territories, and regions to reduce legal, regulatory, sanctions, abuse, network, data protection, crawler-abuse, and local internet-law compliance risk.

1.2 These restrictions are designed to help a personal blog comply with applicable local rules, German and European Union obligations, upstream provider rules, sanctions and export-control obligations, and anti-abuse controls.

1.3 These restrictions are operational compliance measures. They are not a statement about any nationality, ethnicity, language, culture, religion, or individual person.

## 2. Restricted countries and regions

2.1 The following countries and regions are restricted and may be blocked, limited, challenged, rate-limited, or subject to KYC1 review. They are listed alphabetically in English:

- Afghanistan
- Belarus
- China, People's Republic of
- Commonwealth of Independent States
- Cuba
- Democratic People's Republic of Korea
- Hong Kong
- India
- Iran
- Iraq
- Laos
- Macau
- Myanmar
- Nepal
- Pakistan
- Russian Federation
- Thailand
- Vietnam
- The African continent, including all countries and territories geographically located in Africa

2.2 SilentFlare may add, remove, merge, rename, or reclassify restricted regions when required by law, provider rules, sanctions changes, abuse patterns, crawler behavior, security risk, local internet regulations, or operational necessity.

2.3 A country or region may be treated as restricted even if not listed where SilentFlare reasonably determines that access creates unacceptable legal, security, abuse, or operational risk.

## 3. Access controls

3.1 SilentFlare may block, challenge, rate-limit, redirect, cache-limit, robots-limit, feed-limit, comment-limit, or otherwise restrict traffic from restricted regions using IP geolocation, ASN, DNS signals, user agent, crawler behavior, request rate, device signals, referrer signals, language or region signals, prior abuse records, or other risk indicators.

3.2 Geolocation and risk data may be imperfect. A user or crawler operator who is incorrectly blocked may contact SilentFlare and may be asked to complete KYC1 or provide a legitimate-access explanation.

3.3 Access from a restricted region without required review is prohibited even if technical blocking fails.

## 4. Mandatory KYC1 for restricted-region access

4.1 A user, commenter, crawler operator, mirror operator, archive operator, researcher, or automated client materially connected to a restricted region may be required to complete KYC1 before accessing restricted blog functions, comment functions, feed endpoints, API endpoints, archives, high-rate pages, or allowlisted access.

4.2 KYC1 may require one valid government-issued identity document, such as a passport, national identity card, driving licence, or residence permit, and face or liveness verification where reasonably necessary.

4.3 SilentFlare does not use KYC2, KYC3, proof of address, source-of-funds review, or enhanced commercial due diligence for ordinary personal blog access.

4.4 Completion of KYC1 does not guarantee access. SilentFlare may deny, block, rate-limit, or terminate access if risk remains unacceptable.

## 5. Prohibited circumvention

5.1 A user must not hide or misrepresent restricted-region status using VPNs, proxies, residential proxies, data-center proxies, Tor exit nodes, remote desktops, borrowed accounts, false documents, nominee users, false crawler identities, misleading user agents, false addresses, or traffic relays.

5.2 A user must not resell, share, tunnel, proxy, relay, mirror, scrape for redistribution, or provide indirect access to the blog for a person or entity in a restricted region unless SilentFlare gives prior written permission and any required KYC1 has been completed.

5.3 Circumvention may result in blocking, rate limiting, comment removal, feed blocking, crawler blocking, evidence preservation, provider reporting, authority reporting where required, and permanent refusal of future access.

## 6. Change of location or status

6.1 A user seeking allowlisted access must promptly notify SilentFlare if the user's country of residence, operating location, crawler operator, controlling organization, primary access location, or restricted-region connection changes.

6.2 SilentFlare may suspend allowlisting or maintain blocking while reviewing a change of status.

## 7. Exceptions

7.1 SilentFlare may grant a written exception after KYC1, legitimate-use review, crawler review, regional-compliance review, or operational approval.

7.2 Exceptions are discretionary, limited, revocable, non-transferable, and may include rate limits, page limits, feed limits, crawler identification requirements, user-agent requirements, cache requirements, comment limits, additional monitoring, or periodic re-verification.

7.3 An exception granted to one user, IP address, crawler, ASN, organization, or region does not create a right for any other user.

## 8. No obligation to provide access

8.1 SilentFlare is not obligated to provide blog access, comment access, feed access, crawler access, archive access, API access, or allowlisting to any restricted-region user.

8.2 SilentFlare may refuse, block, rate-limit, restrict, remove, or terminate access where legal uncertainty, provider risk, sanctions risk, crawler-abuse risk, spam risk, data protection risk, security risk, or operational burden is unacceptable.
