# Restricted Countries and Regions

## 1. Purpose

1.1 SilentFlare restricts access from certain countries, territories, and regions to reduce legal, regulatory, sanctions, payment, abuse, network, data protection, and local compliance risk.

1.2 These restrictions are designed to help SilentFlare comply with applicable local rules, German and European Union obligations, upstream provider rules, payment processor rules, sanctions and export control obligations, and operational risk controls.

1.3 These restrictions are operational compliance measures. They are not a statement about any nationality, ethnicity, language, culture, or individual person.

## 2. Restricted countries and regions

2.1 The following countries and regions are restricted and may be blocked, limited, or subject to mandatory verification. They are listed alphabetically in English:

- Afghanistan
- Belarus
- China, People's Republic of
- Commonwealth of Independent States
- Cuba
- Democratic People's Republic of Korea
- Hong Kong
- India
- Iran
- Iraq
- Laos
- Macau
- Myanmar
- Nepal
- Pakistan
- Russian Federation
- Thailand
- Vietnam
- The African continent, including all countries and territories geographically located in Africa

2.2 SilentFlare may add, remove, merge, rename, or reclassify restricted regions at any time when required by law, provider rules, sanctions changes, payment processor requirements, abuse patterns, security risk, or operational necessity.

2.3 A country or region may be treated as restricted even if it is not listed, where SilentFlare reasonably determines that service provision creates unacceptable risk.

## 3. Access controls

3.1 SilentFlare may block, challenge, rate-limit, redirect, or restrict traffic from restricted regions using IP geolocation, payment country, billing address, identity document country, phone country, device signals, account behavior, ASN, DNS signals, or other risk indicators.

3.2 Geolocation data may be imperfect. A user who is incorrectly blocked may contact SilentFlare and may be asked to complete verification.

3.3 Access from a restricted region without required approval is prohibited even if technical blocking fails.

## 4. Mandatory KYC for restricted-region users

4.1 A user located in, ordinarily resident in, incorporated in, operating from, routing traffic through, or otherwise materially connected to a restricted region must complete at least KYC Level 1 before accessing restricted service functions.

4.2 KYC Level 1 requires submission and verification of at least one valid government-issued identity document, such as a passport, national identity card, driving licence, or residence permit, together with face verification when required.

4.3 SilentFlare may require KYC Level 2, including proof of address, or KYC Level 3, including enhanced due diligence, source-of-funds review, source-of-wealth review, corporate ownership review, sanctions screening, business purpose review, or manual approval.

4.4 Completion of KYC does not guarantee service access. SilentFlare may deny or terminate access if risk remains unacceptable.

## 5. Prohibited circumvention

5.1 A user must not hide or misrepresent restricted-region status using VPNs, proxies, residential proxies, data-center proxies, Tor exit nodes, remote desktops, borrowed accounts, false documents, nominee users, false addresses, shell companies, forwarding addresses, virtual cards, or misleading payment instruments.

5.2 A user must not resell, share, sublicense, tunnel, proxy, relay, or provide indirect access to any person or entity in a restricted region unless SilentFlare gives prior written permission and any required verification has been completed.

5.3 Circumvention may result in immediate suspension, termination, evidence preservation, denial of refund, reporting to relevant providers or authorities, and permanent refusal of future service.

## 6. Change of location or status

6.1 The user must promptly notify SilentFlare if the user's country of residence, business registration, billing address, nationality, operating location, control person, beneficial owner, or primary access location changes in a way that may affect restricted-region status.

6.2 SilentFlare may suspend service while reviewing a change of status.

## 7. Exceptions

7.1 SilentFlare may grant a written exception for a restricted-region user after verification, risk review, and operational approval.

7.2 Exceptions are discretionary, limited, revocable, non-transferable, and may include feature limits, payment limits, traffic limits, additional monitoring, or periodic re-verification.

7.3 An exception granted to one user does not create a right for any other user.

## 8. No obligation to serve

8.1 SilentFlare is not obligated to provide service to any restricted-region user.

8.2 SilentFlare may refuse, suspend, terminate, or limit service where legal uncertainty, provider risk, fraud risk, sanctions risk, abuse risk, payment risk, data protection risk, or operational burden is unacceptable.
