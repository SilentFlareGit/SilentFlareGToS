# Legal, Abuse, and Compliance Contact Rules

## 1. Purpose

1.1 This policy explains how users and third parties should contact SilentFlare about support, abuse, legal notices, compliance requests, privacy requests, billing disputes, and security reports.

1.2 Correct routing helps SilentFlare respond faster and preserve evidence properly.

## 2. General support

2.1 General account, billing, technical, and service questions should be sent through the designated support channel shown on the website, dashboard, repository, or order page.

2.2 Support requests should include the account identifier, service identifier, relevant dates, screenshots if useful, logs if safe to share, and a clear description of the issue.

2.3 Users must not include passwords, private keys, seed phrases, full payment card numbers, or unnecessary sensitive data in support messages.

## 3. Abuse reports

3.1 Abuse reports should include the affected URL, IP address, domain, timestamp with timezone, logs, headers, screenshots, transaction identifiers, or other evidence.

3.2 Reports involving malware, phishing, spam, DDoS, unauthorized access, copyright infringement, child safety, threats, fraud, or illegal content should be clearly labeled.

3.3 SilentFlare may prioritize reports based on severity, evidence quality, legal risk, user risk, and provider deadlines.

## 4. Security reports

4.1 Security researchers should report vulnerabilities responsibly and avoid destructive testing, data access, persistence, lateral movement, social engineering, spam, service degradation, or public disclosure before remediation.

4.2 Reports should include reproduction steps, impact, affected endpoint, proof of concept, and suggested remediation.

4.3 SilentFlare may restrict or deny testing that causes harm, violates law, exposes private data, or affects third parties.

## 5. Legal notices

5.1 Legal notices should clearly identify the sender, legal basis, affected service, requested action, evidence, jurisdiction, deadline, and contact details.

5.2 SilentFlare may require additional verification before acting on a legal notice.

5.3 SilentFlare may reject incomplete, abusive, fraudulent, unclear, overbroad, or legally defective notices.

## 6. Privacy and data rights requests

6.1 Privacy requests should identify the account and the specific right being exercised, such as access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, or export.

6.2 SilentFlare may require identity verification before fulfilling a privacy request.

6.3 SilentFlare may refuse or limit requests where permitted by law, including where data must be retained for abuse prevention, fraud prevention, legal compliance, dispute resolution, or legal claims.

## 7. Billing disputes

7.1 Billing disputes should include invoice number, payment date, payment method, charged amount, expected amount, and explanation.

7.2 The user should contact SilentFlare before initiating a chargeback, where practical.

7.3 Filing a chargeback may cause temporary suspension while the dispute is reviewed.

## 8. Communications from SilentFlare

8.1 SilentFlare may send notices by email, dashboard message, website notice, repository update, support ticket, or other reasonable communication method.

8.2 The user is responsible for keeping contact information current and monitoring relevant communication channels.

8.3 Notices are deemed received when sent, posted, or made available, unless mandatory law requires otherwise.

## 9. Abusive communications

9.1 Users must not threaten, harass, spam, dox, impersonate, blackmail, or abuse SilentFlare staff, users, providers, or reporters.

9.2 SilentFlare may limit support, close tickets, suspend accounts, or terminate service for abusive communications.

## 10. Emergency matters

10.1 Emergency reports involving imminent harm, active compromise, severe abuse, child safety, terrorism, credible threats, or major security incidents should be clearly marked as urgent and include immediately actionable evidence.

10.2 SilentFlare may take emergency action before completing full review where necessary to reduce harm.
