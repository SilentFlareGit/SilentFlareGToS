# Acceptable Use Policy

## 1. General rule

1.1 The user may access the SilentFlare blog only for lawful, authorized, non-abusive, and policy-compliant purposes.

1.2 The user is responsible for all comments, messages, crawler traffic, scripts, automation, user agents, mirrors, archives, quotations, reposts, and systems used to access the blog.

1.3 SilentFlare may investigate, restrict, block, rate-limit, remove, or deny activity that creates legal risk, security risk, infrastructure risk, provider risk, privacy risk, copyright risk, reputation risk, crawler-abuse risk, or harm to third parties.

## 2. Illegal content and activity

2.1 The user must not use the blog, comments, contact channels, feeds, public files, or repositories for unlawful activity under German law, European Union law, the law of the user's location, the law of affected third parties, or provider rules.

2.2 Prohibited activity includes fraud, scams, phishing, identity theft, credential theft, malware, botnets, ransomware, unauthorized access, card testing, money laundering, sanctions evasion, terrorist content, child sexual abuse material, trafficking, extortion, illegal gambling, unlawful harassment, and copyright infringement.

2.3 The user must not knowingly assist, facilitate, advertise, fund, conceal, or profit from prohibited activity.

## 3. Comment and communication rules

3.1 Users must not post or send spam, threats, harassment, doxing, impersonation, defamation, illegal content, malware links, phishing links, unlawful extremist content, sexual content involving minors, or personal data published without lawful basis.

3.2 Users must not impersonate SilentFlare, staff, other users, public officials, companies, projects, crawlers, researchers, authorities, or third parties.

3.3 SilentFlare may edit, refuse, hide, remove, or moderate comments and contact messages for safety, legality, readability, spam control, or relevance.

## 4. Crawler, scraping, and automation rules

4.1 Crawlers must identify themselves truthfully, use a stable user agent, provide a reachable contact where reasonable, respect robots instructions where published, and keep request rates reasonable.

4.2 The user must not scrape at abusive scale, bypass rate limits, ignore blocks, rotate IP addresses to evade limits, spoof search-engine identity, collect personal data unlawfully, or build mirrors that create legal, privacy, copyright, or operational risk.

4.3 SilentFlare may block, rate-limit, challenge, cache-limit, feed-limit, or require KYC1 from crawlers, bots, scrapers, mirrors, archives, automated research tools, AI data collectors, or other automated clients.

## 5. Security abuse

5.1 The user must not scan, probe, attack, overload, exploit, bypass, intercept, scrape abusively, or disrupt SilentFlare systems or third-party systems without authorization.

5.2 The user must not run malware, droppers, exploit kits, phishing kits, spam tools, credential stuffing tools, brute-force tools, DDoS tools, botnet controllers, command-and-control systems, or unauthorized vulnerability exploitation against or through the blog.

5.3 Security research is allowed only if it is non-destructive, limited, authorized where required, does not access private data, does not degrade service, and is promptly reported through the appropriate contact channel.

## 6. Intellectual property and quotation

6.1 Unless a page states otherwise, blog content is provided for reading and ordinary fair quotation. Copying, reposting, training, scraping, mirroring, translating, or commercial reuse may require permission depending on applicable law and the content involved.

6.2 Users must preserve attribution when quoting SilentFlare content and must not misrepresent context, authorship, date, or meaning.

6.3 Users must not remove copyright notices, author information, license notices, or source links where they are provided.

## 7. Privacy and personal data abuse

7.1 The user must not collect, process, publish, sell, leak, scrape, or expose personal data from the blog, comments, logs, contact channels, or connected pages without a lawful basis and required notices.

7.2 The user must not dox, stalk, harass, blackmail, impersonate, or target individuals using personal data.

7.3 The user must not submit unnecessary sensitive data to comments or contact channels.

## 8. Resource abuse

8.1 The user must not consume excessive bandwidth, request capacity, feed capacity, comment capacity, storage, support time, or network resources in a way that harms the blog, infrastructure, providers, or other users.

8.2 SilentFlare may apply quotas, throttling, rate limits, bot challenges, cache rules, robots rules, IP blocks, ASN blocks, region blocks, or manual review.

8.3 Attempts to bypass limits through multiple IPs, VPNs, proxies, automation, misleading crawler identity, or technical manipulation are prohibited.

## 9. Reporting and remediation

9.1 SilentFlare may require the user to stop traffic, reduce crawling, identify the crawler, remove copied content, correct attribution, delete unlawful copies, rotate credentials, or provide remediation evidence.

9.2 The user must cooperate promptly and truthfully.

9.3 Failure to remediate may result in blocking, reporting, evidence preservation, comment removal, feed restriction, or refusal of future access.
