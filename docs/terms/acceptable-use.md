# Acceptable Use Policy

## 1. General rule

1.1 The user may use SilentFlare services only for lawful, authorized, non-abusive, and policy-compliant purposes.

1.2 The user is responsible for all content, traffic, scripts, automations, credentials, users, invitees, integrations, and systems connected to the user's account.

1.3 SilentFlare may investigate, restrict, suspend, or terminate activity that creates legal risk, security risk, infrastructure risk, provider risk, reputation risk, payment risk, or harm to other users or third parties.

## 2. Illegal content and activity

2.1 The user must not use the service for unlawful activity under German law, European Union law, the law of the user's location, the law of affected third parties, or provider rules.

2.2 Prohibited activity includes fraud, scams, phishing, identity theft, credential theft, malware, botnets, ransomware, unauthorized access, card testing, money laundering, sanctions evasion, terrorist content, child sexual abuse material, trafficking, extortion, illegal gambling, and unlawful harassment.

2.3 The user must not knowingly assist, facilitate, advertise, fund, conceal, or profit from prohibited activity.

## 3. Security abuse

3.1 The user must not scan, probe, attack, overload, exploit, bypass, intercept, scrape, or disrupt SilentFlare systems or third-party systems without authorization.

3.2 The user must not run malware, droppers, exploit kits, phishing kits, spam tools, credential stuffing tools, brute-force tools, DDoS tools, botnet controllers, command-and-control servers, or unauthorized vulnerability exploitation.

3.3 Security research is allowed only if it is non-destructive, limited, authorized, does not access private data, does not degrade service, and is promptly reported through the appropriate contact channel.

## 4. Network and hosting abuse

4.1 The user must not generate abusive traffic, spam, mass unsolicited messages, open proxies, public relays, traffic amplification, port scanning, DDoS traffic, scraping at abusive scale, or traffic that causes upstream complaints.

4.2 The user must not use the service to bypass rate limits, access controls, paywalls, regional restrictions, account bans, sanctions controls, or provider restrictions.

4.3 The user must not host, mirror, index, transmit, redirect, or proxy content that is unlawful, abusive, infringing, or designed to evade enforcement.

## 5. Intellectual property

5.1 The user must not use the service to infringe copyright, trademark, database rights, trade secrets, privacy rights, publicity rights, or other third-party rights.

5.2 The user must remove or disable infringing content promptly after notice.

5.3 Repeated infringement may result in termination.

## 6. Privacy and personal data abuse

6.1 The user must not collect, process, publish, sell, leak, scrape, or expose personal data without a lawful basis and required notices.

6.2 The user must not dox, stalk, harass, blackmail, impersonate, or target individuals using personal data.

6.3 The user must not upload special category data, health data, biometric data, government identifiers, children's data, or highly sensitive data unless the service specifically permits it and the user has a lawful basis.

## 7. Harmful, deceptive, or misleading conduct

7.1 The user must not impersonate SilentFlare, its staff, other users, public officials, companies, or third parties.

7.2 The user must not misrepresent ownership, affiliation, authorization, identity, location, legal status, payment status, or compliance status.

7.3 The user must not use the service for deceptive advertising, fake reviews, spam campaigns, social manipulation, or other fraudulent influence activity.

## 8. Resource abuse

8.1 The user must not consume excessive CPU, memory, storage, bandwidth, API capacity, email capacity, support time, or network resources in a way that harms the service or other users.

8.2 SilentFlare may apply quotas, throttling, fair-use limits, storage limits, traffic limits, process limits, billing limits, or manual review.

8.3 Attempts to bypass limits through multiple accounts, automation, or technical manipulation are prohibited.

## 9. Resale and third-party access

9.1 The user must not resell, sublicense, rent, lend, share, proxy, tunnel, or provide managed access to the service unless SilentFlare expressly permits it in writing.

9.2 The user remains responsible for all activity by customers, invitees, team members, contractors, bots, scripts, or downstream users.

## 10. Reporting and remediation

10.1 SilentFlare may require the user to investigate abuse reports, provide logs, remove content, patch systems, rotate credentials, disable integrations, or stop traffic.

10.2 The user must cooperate promptly and truthfully.

10.3 Failure to remediate may result in immediate suspension or termination.
