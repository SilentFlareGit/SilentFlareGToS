# Germany and European Union Compliance

## 1. Infrastructure location and compliance baseline

1.1 SilentFlare services may be hosted on servers, networks, or infrastructure located in Germany or the European Union.

1.2 Because the infrastructure may be located in Germany or the European Union, every user must comply with German law, European Union law, applicable local law, hosting provider policies, network abuse rules, sanctions rules, export control rules, and lawful requests from competent authorities.

1.3 The user may not claim that a law in the user's own country permits conduct that is illegal, restricted, or unsafe under German law, European Union law, provider rules, or these terms.

## 2. Compliance with local laws

2.1 The user is solely responsible for determining whether the service may legally be accessed, purchased, used, exported, imported, encrypted, operated, or resold in the user's jurisdiction.

2.2 SilentFlare may restrict, block, suspend, or terminate service where access from a jurisdiction creates unreasonable legal, regulatory, security, sanctions, export control, consumer protection, taxation, payment, or provider compliance risk.

2.3 SilentFlare may refuse service where the user cannot provide information reasonably required to assess legal or compliance risk.

## 3. German and EU legal restrictions

3.1 The user must not use the service for activity prohibited by German or European Union law, including unlawful hate speech, unlawful extremist content, terrorist content, child sexual abuse material, unlawful harassment, fraud, malware, unauthorized access, identity theft, phishing, illegal gambling, copyright infringement, trafficking, sanctions evasion, or illegal financial activity.

3.2 The user must not use the service to publish, store, process, transmit, index, mirror, proxy, relay, or facilitate unlawful content or traffic.

3.3 The user must not use the service to evade blocking, filtering, monitoring, licensing, age restriction, sanctions, export controls, court orders, administrative orders, or provider enforcement.

## 4. Sanctions and export control

4.1 The user represents that the user is not subject to sanctions, asset freezes, travel bans, trade restrictions, export control restrictions, or other prohibitions that would make service provision unlawful or high risk.

4.2 The user must not provide the service, access credentials, API keys, infrastructure, content, technical data, or derived service access to any sanctioned person, entity, region, organization, or prohibited end user.

4.3 The user must not use the service to support military end uses, prohibited surveillance, weapons development, cyber abuse, state-sponsored attacks, sanctions evasion, or other restricted end uses.

4.4 SilentFlare may screen users, traffic, metadata, payment information, identity documents, and account behavior for sanctions and export control risk.

## 5. Anti-abuse and provider compliance

5.1 SilentFlare may be required to comply with upstream hosting, network, registrar, payment, identity verification, email, CDN, or infrastructure provider policies.

5.2 If an upstream provider reports abuse, requires suspension, requests verification, imposes traffic limits, or terminates infrastructure, SilentFlare may take immediate action against affected accounts without prior notice.

5.3 The user must cooperate with abuse investigations and must provide accurate logs, configurations, contact details, identification, and remediation evidence when requested.

## 6. Lawful requests and notices

6.1 SilentFlare may respond to lawful requests from courts, regulators, police, tax authorities, sanctions authorities, data protection authorities, hosting providers, payment processors, or other competent bodies.

6.2 SilentFlare may preserve, disclose, restrict, or remove information where legally required or where reasonably necessary to protect the service, other users, third parties, or SilentFlare.

6.3 SilentFlare may notify the user of a lawful request when legally permitted, operationally safe, and reasonably practical.

## 7. User warranties

7.1 The user warrants that all registration, KYC, billing, tax, residency, nationality, address, corporate, and contact information provided to SilentFlare is accurate, current, complete, and not misleading.

7.2 The user warrants that use of the service will not expose SilentFlare to unlawful activity, licensing violations, sanctions violations, tax evasion, consumer protection violations, privacy violations, or provider policy violations.

## 8. Compliance review

8.1 SilentFlare may review any account before or after activation.

8.2 SilentFlare may delay activation, require additional KYC, request source-of-funds information, limit features, restrict regions, disable API keys, block traffic, suspend service, or terminate accounts if compliance risk is unresolved.

8.3 Failure to complete a compliance request within the requested time may result in suspension or termination.

## 9. No circumvention

9.1 The user must not evade compliance controls using VPNs, proxies, false addresses, borrowed documents, shell accounts, nominee users, synthetic identities, resellers, account sharing, remote desktops, traffic relays, or misleading payment methods.

9.2 If SilentFlare detects circumvention, SilentFlare may immediately suspend or terminate service, preserve evidence, deny refunds, and report the matter where required or appropriate.
