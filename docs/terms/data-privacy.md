# Data Protection, Privacy, and Retention

## 1. Scope

1.1 This policy explains operational privacy and data handling rules for SilentFlare services.

1.2 It applies to account data, billing data, KYC data, support messages, technical logs, security logs, abuse reports, service content, metadata, payment metadata, and communication records.

1.3 This policy is an operational draft and should be reviewed against the GDPR, German data protection law, and any applicable sector-specific rules before commercial use.

## 2. Data categories

2.1 SilentFlare may process account information, such as name, username, email address, phone number, account identifiers, login records, language, region, and account settings.

2.2 SilentFlare may process billing information, such as invoices, payment status, billing address, tax information, transaction identifiers, payment provider metadata, refunds, chargebacks, and risk signals.

2.3 SilentFlare may process KYC information, such as identity documents, face verification, address documents, corporate documents, beneficial ownership details, screening results, and manual review notes.

2.4 SilentFlare may process technical information, such as IP addresses, device information, browser information, user agent, API requests, DNS records, server logs, access logs, error logs, traffic metadata, and security events.

2.5 SilentFlare may process user content where necessary to provide, secure, support, investigate, or comply with obligations related to the service.

## 3. Purposes

3.1 SilentFlare may process data to provide the service, authenticate users, operate accounts, process payments, provide support, prevent abuse, investigate incidents, comply with law, enforce terms, improve reliability, and protect users and third parties.

3.2 SilentFlare may process data to detect fraud, sanctions risk, restricted-region circumvention, malware, phishing, DDoS activity, account takeover, spam, policy violations, and payment abuse.

## 4. Legal bases and user responsibility

4.1 SilentFlare should identify and document applicable legal bases before commercial launch, including contract performance, legitimate interests, legal obligation, consent where required, and vital or public interests where applicable.

4.2 The user is responsible for having a lawful basis for any personal data the user uploads, stores, transmits, or processes through the service.

4.3 The user must not use the service to process personal data in a way that violates data protection law.

## 5. Sharing and processors

5.1 SilentFlare may share data with hosting providers, payment processors, identity verification providers, fraud prevention providers, security providers, email providers, analytics providers, storage providers, legal advisors, auditors, and support vendors where necessary.

5.2 SilentFlare may disclose data to authorities, courts, regulators, law enforcement, sanctions authorities, tax authorities, or providers when legally required or reasonably necessary to protect the service.

5.3 SilentFlare should use appropriate data processing agreements where required.

## 6. International transfers

6.1 Data may be processed in countries where SilentFlare, its providers, or its infrastructure operate.

6.2 If data is transferred outside the European Economic Area, SilentFlare should assess and implement appropriate transfer mechanisms where required.

## 7. Retention

7.1 SilentFlare retains data only as long as reasonably necessary for service operation, billing, KYC, security, abuse prevention, legal compliance, dispute resolution, audit, and enforcement.

7.2 Security logs, abuse records, payment records, KYC records, and account enforcement records may be retained after account closure where necessary to prevent repeat abuse, resolve disputes, comply with law, or defend legal claims.

7.3 SilentFlare may delete inactive, expired, suspended, or terminated account data after reasonable retention periods, unless law or operational needs require retention.

## 8. User rights

8.1 Users may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or export personal data under applicable law.

8.2 SilentFlare may require identity verification before fulfilling a data rights request.

8.3 SilentFlare may refuse or limit a request where permitted by law, including where retention is required for security, legal compliance, fraud prevention, abuse prevention, or legal claims.

## 9. Security

9.1 SilentFlare will use reasonable technical and organizational measures appropriate to the size, nature, and risk of the service.

9.2 No online service is completely secure. The user is responsible for securing credentials, endpoints, devices, integrations, backups, and user-side systems.

## 10. Breach handling

10.1 SilentFlare may investigate suspected data breaches, preserve evidence, suspend access, rotate credentials, notify affected users, notify regulators, and notify providers where required or appropriate.

10.2 The user must promptly notify SilentFlare of suspected unauthorized access or personal data incidents involving the service.
