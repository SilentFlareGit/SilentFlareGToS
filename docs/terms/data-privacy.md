## 1. Scope

1.1 This policy explains operational privacy and data handling rules for the SilentFlare personal blog.

1.2 It applies to access logs, security logs, comments, contact messages, feed requests, crawler traffic, abuse reports, KYC1 review data, donation metadata where applicable, and technical metadata connected to the blog.

1.3 This policy is an operational draft and should be reviewed against the GDPR, German data protection law, ePrivacy rules, and any applicable local rules before relying on it formally.

## 2. Data categories

2.1 SilentFlare may process access information, such as IP address, timestamp, requested URL, referrer, user agent, HTTP headers, language signals, device or browser metadata, ASN, approximate geolocation, and error logs.

2.2 SilentFlare may process comment and contact information, such as display name, email address, website URL, message content, moderation status, spam score, submitted metadata, and communication records.

2.3 SilentFlare may process crawler and automation information, such as user agent, request rate, requested paths, robots behavior, IP range, ASN, cache behavior, block status, allowlist status, and crawler contact details.

2.4 SilentFlare may process limited KYC1 information where required for anti-abuse or restricted-region review, such as identity document data, face or liveness data where necessary, appeal statements, IP information, risk signals, and manual review notes.

2.5 SilentFlare may process donation or voluntary support metadata where applicable, such as transaction identifiers, amount, currency, payment status, provider metadata, refund status, and fraud signals.

## 3. Purposes

3.1 SilentFlare may process data to operate the blog, deliver pages, maintain security, moderate comments, respond to messages, prevent spam, detect crawler abuse, investigate incidents, comply with law, enforce terms, and protect users and third parties.

3.2 SilentFlare may process data to detect malicious crawling, scraping at abusive scale, spam comments, malware links, phishing, DDoS activity, account takeover, restricted-region circumvention, sanctions risk, and policy violations.

## 4. Legal bases and user responsibility

4.1 Depending on the context, processing may rely on legitimate interests, contract or pre-contract steps where applicable, legal obligation, consent where required, or another lawful basis under applicable law.

4.2 The user is responsible for having a lawful basis for any personal data the user submits, quotes, posts, mirrors, scrapes, indexes, stores, or republishes.

4.3 The user must not use the blog to process personal data in a way that violates data protection law.

## 5. Sharing and processors

5.1 SilentFlare may share data with hosting providers, DNS providers, CDN providers, repository providers, email providers, comment providers, analytics providers, security providers, anti-abuse providers, identity verification providers for KYC1, payment providers for donations, legal advisors, and support vendors where necessary.

5.2 SilentFlare may disclose data to authorities, courts, regulators, law enforcement, sanctions authorities, data protection authorities, or providers when legally required or reasonably necessary to protect the blog.

5.3 SilentFlare should use appropriate data processing agreements where required.

## 6. International transfers

6.1 Data may be processed in countries where SilentFlare, its providers, or its infrastructure operate.

6.2 If data is transferred outside the European Economic Area, SilentFlare should assess and implement appropriate transfer mechanisms where required.

## 7. Retention

7.1 SilentFlare retains data only as long as reasonably necessary for blog operation, security, moderation, anti-abuse, crawler control, KYC1 review, legal compliance, dispute resolution, audit, and enforcement.

7.2 Security logs, abuse records, block records, KYC1 review records, payment metadata, and enforcement records may be retained after access ends where necessary to prevent repeat abuse, resolve disputes, comply with law, satisfy provider obligations, or defend legal claims.

7.3 SilentFlare may delete comments, messages, logs, inactive access records, expired allowlist records, or blocked-user records after reasonable retention periods unless law or operational needs require retention.

## 8. User rights

8.1 Users may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict, object to, or export personal data under applicable law.

8.2 SilentFlare may require identity verification, including KYC1 where appropriate, before fulfilling a data-rights request.

8.3 SilentFlare may refuse or limit a request where permitted by law, including where retention is required for security, legal compliance, fraud prevention, abuse prevention, provider obligations, or legal claims.

## 9. Cookies, analytics, and embeds

9.1 The blog may use cookies, local storage, analytics, embedded media, CDN logs, anti-spam tools, or third-party widgets where disclosed or technically necessary.

9.2 Third-party embeds may process user data under their own policies.

9.3 SilentFlare should provide additional cookie or consent notices where required by law.

## 10. Security

10.1 SilentFlare will use reasonable technical and organizational measures appropriate to the size, personal nature, and risk of the blog.

10.2 No online site is completely secure. Users should avoid submitting unnecessary sensitive data through comments, contact forms, or public channels.

## 11. Breach handling

11.1 SilentFlare may investigate suspected data breaches, preserve evidence, block access, rotate credentials, remove comments, notify affected users, notify regulators, and notify providers where required or appropriate.

11.2 The user must promptly notify SilentFlare of suspected unauthorized access, personal data exposure, comment compromise, or abuse involving the blog.
