# Moderation, Blocking, and Enforcement

## 1. Enforcement purpose

1.1 SilentFlare enforces these terms to protect the blog, readers, third parties, infrastructure, providers, legal compliance, privacy, security, and service reputation.

1.2 Enforcement may be automated, manual, provider-driven, complaint-driven, authority-driven, crawler-driven, spam-driven, or risk-based.

## 2. Investigation

2.1 SilentFlare may investigate abuse reports, spam, malicious crawling, scraping at abusive scale, provider complaints, security alerts, KYC1 inconsistencies, sanctions signals, restricted-region signals, traffic anomalies, copyright notices, privacy complaints, and suspected violations.

2.2 During investigation, SilentFlare may review comments, contact messages, logs, traffic metadata, IP addresses, ASNs, user agents, feed requests, crawler behavior, KYC1 data, provider reports, and other relevant information.

2.3 SilentFlare may request information from the user, crawler operator, commenter, or reporter. Failure to respond truthfully and promptly may result in continued blocking or restriction.

## 3. Immediate action

3.1 SilentFlare may take immediate action without prior notice where necessary to stop harm, comply with law, satisfy provider requirements, prevent abuse, protect security, preserve evidence, reduce crawler load, or reduce legal risk.

3.2 Immediate action may include blocking traffic, rate-limiting, disabling comments, removing comments, disabling feeds, revoking tokens, blocking IPs or ASNs, blocking countries or regions, requiring KYC1, removing content, or refusing contact.

## 4. Notice

4.1 SilentFlare may provide notice by email, page notice, repository notice, contact-channel reply, feed notice, website notice, or other reasonable method.

4.2 SilentFlare may withhold notice where notice would create risk, reveal detection methods, violate law, compromise an investigation, identify a reporter, or enable further abuse.

## 5. Blocking and restriction

5.1 Blocking may be temporary or indefinite.

5.2 During blocking or restriction, the user may lose access to pages, comments, feeds, APIs, archives, contact channels, allowlisting, or other blog functions.

5.3 SilentFlare may require remediation, KYC1, traffic reduction, crawler identification, attribution correction, content removal, security fixes, abuse explanation, written commitments, or provider approval before restoring access.

## 6. Comment moderation and content removal

6.1 SilentFlare may reject, hide, edit, remove, quarantine, or preserve comments and submitted content where required by law, provider rules, abuse policy, spam control, privacy protection, copyright claims, or editorial judgment.

6.2 SilentFlare is not required to host comments, links, user submissions, crawler results, mirrors, or third-party copies.

6.3 SilentFlare may preserve evidence of abuse, fraud, KYC1 fraud, scraping abuse, security incidents, or legal requests after access is blocked.

## 7. Termination of interaction

7.1 SilentFlare may permanently refuse interaction if the user violates these terms, fails KYC1, creates unacceptable risk, engages in abuse, provides false information, violates law, causes provider enforcement, or repeatedly evades blocks.

7.2 Permanent refusal may include refusal of comments, email replies, contact requests, crawler allowlisting, restricted-region exceptions, and future access appeals.

## 8. Appeals

8.1 The user may request review of a block, comment removal, crawler restriction, KYC1 decision, or other enforcement action by contacting the designated channel.

8.2 The appeal should include relevant dates, affected URLs, IP addresses, ASNs, crawler user agent, account or comment identifier, explanation, evidence, remediation steps, and any documents requested by SilentFlare.

8.3 SilentFlare may deny appeals that are abusive, incomplete, misleading, repetitive, high-risk, or inconsistent with regional-compliance controls.

## 9. Repeat abuse

9.1 SilentFlare may permanently block users, devices, payment methods, identity documents, IP ranges, ASNs, domains, organizations, user agents, crawlers, mirrors, or related access patterns associated with repeat abuse.

9.2 SilentFlare may treat related users, crawlers, IP ranges, domains, accounts, or organizations as one actor for enforcement where there is shared ownership, control, infrastructure, content, behavior, or evasion pattern.
