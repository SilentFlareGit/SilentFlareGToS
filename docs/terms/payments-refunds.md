# Payments, Taxes, Refunds, and Chargebacks

## 1. Prices and billing

1.1 Prices, currencies, billing periods, plan limits, setup fees, renewal fees, usage fees, taxes, and payment methods may be displayed on the website, dashboard, invoice, order page, or written quotation.

1.2 SilentFlare may change prices for future orders, renewals, upgrades, or new billing periods.

1.3 The user is responsible for reviewing all order details before payment.

## 2. Taxes

2.1 Prices may exclude taxes unless expressly stated otherwise.

2.2 The user is responsible for all applicable taxes, duties, levies, withholding, currency conversion fees, card fees, bank fees, and payment processor fees.

2.3 SilentFlare may collect tax information, billing address, country information, VAT information, or other data needed for tax compliance.

## 3. Payment authorization

3.1 By providing a payment method, the user authorizes SilentFlare or its payment processor to charge applicable fees, renewals, usage charges, taxes, and other amounts due.

3.2 The user represents that the payment method is authorized, lawful, not stolen, not fraudulent, not used to evade sanctions, and not used to hide restricted-region status.

3.3 SilentFlare may delay activation until payment clears and risk review is complete.

## 4. Renewals

4.1 Services may renew automatically if the order, dashboard, invoice, or service description states that renewal applies.

4.2 The user is responsible for cancelling renewal before the renewal date if the user does not want continued service.

4.3 Failure to use the service does not automatically cancel renewal or create a refund right.

## 5. Failed payments

5.1 If payment fails, is reversed, is disputed, is suspected fraudulent, or is not received on time, SilentFlare may suspend, limit, downgrade, delete, or terminate service.

5.2 SilentFlare may require a different payment method, KYC, manual review, or prepayment before reactivation.

5.3 The user remains responsible for unpaid amounts and reasonable collection costs where permitted by law.

## 6. Refunds

6.1 Refund eligibility depends on the specific service, plan, written order terms, consumer law, and payment processor rules.

6.2 Unless SilentFlare states otherwise in writing, setup fees, domain fees, third-party fees, consumed usage, custom work, manual review fees, KYC-related fees, abuse-related fees, and already-incurred provider costs are non-refundable.

6.3 SilentFlare may deny refunds where service is suspended or terminated for abuse, fraud, sanctions risk, restricted-region circumvention, chargeback abuse, false KYC, illegal activity, or violation of these terms.

6.4 Refunds, if granted, may be returned to the original payment method and may be reduced by taxes, processor fees, currency conversion losses, usage, or unpaid amounts where permitted by law.

## 7. Consumer rights

7.1 If mandatory consumer protection law grants the user non-waivable rights, those rights apply despite any conflicting clause.

7.2 Digital services, custom services, immediate performance, consumed usage, or business accounts may be treated differently under applicable law.

7.3 Nothing in this policy is intended to remove rights that cannot legally be waived.

## 8. Chargebacks and disputes

8.1 The user should contact SilentFlare before filing a chargeback so the issue can be investigated.

8.2 Filing a chargeback, payment dispute, or reversal may result in immediate suspension while ownership, fraud risk, and payment status are reviewed.

8.3 Fraudulent or abusive chargebacks may result in termination, denial of future service, evidence submission to processors, and reporting where appropriate.

## 9. Credits

9.1 SilentFlare may issue credits at its discretion.

9.2 Credits have no cash value, are not transferable, may expire, and may be limited to specified services.

9.3 Credits may be revoked for abuse, fraud, chargebacks, or account termination.

## 10. Billing errors

10.1 The user must report billing errors promptly.

10.2 SilentFlare may correct billing errors, undercharges, overcharges, tax errors, or invoice mistakes.

10.3 SilentFlare may issue corrected invoices where required or appropriate.
